### Narration

Cross-examination preparation remains one of the most time-consuming tasks in litigation.

### Narration

The process begins when a lawyer uploads or pastes a witness statement.

CrossEx AI first extracts the material facts contained within the narrative.

These facts are organized into a structured timeline so that events can be reviewed chronologically.

The system then evaluates the narrative for inconsistencies, omissions, and credibility concerns.

Based on those findings, CrossEx AI generates targeted cross-examination questions designed to clarify disputed facts and challenge conflicting statements.

Every generated question remains reviewable and editable before use.

Human review is always required.

### Narration

Let's look at a simple example.

A witness states:

"I arrived at the intersection at approximately 8:00 PM."

Later in the same statement, the witness says:

"I reached the location around 7:45 PM."

CrossEx AI extracts both facts and places them into the event timeline.

The system immediately identifies a timing inconsistency.

It then generates a suggested cross-examination question.

"You previously stated that you arrived at the intersection at approximately 8:00 PM. Why did you later state that you reached the location around 7:45 PM?"

Instead of manually searching through pages of testimony, the lawyer receives a structured questioning opportunity within seconds.

The same workflow can be applied to witness statements, affidavits, complaints, and deposition transcripts.
