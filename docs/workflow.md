# CrossEx AI Workflow

## Overview

CrossEx AI is designed around a single objective:

Transform witness statements into structured cross-examination preparation.

Rather than acting as a general-purpose legal assistant, CrossEx AI guides lawyers through a focused litigation workflow that combines AI-assisted analysis with human review.

---

# Workflow Summary

```text
Witness Statement
        ↓
Fact Extraction
        ↓
Timeline Analysis
        ↓
Contradiction Detection
        ↓
Question Generation
        ↓
Lawyer Review
        ↓
Export
```

Every stage produces reviewable outputs before moving to the next stage.

---

# Step 1 – Upload Witness Statement

The workflow begins when a lawyer uploads or pastes a witness statement into CrossEx AI.

Supported document types include:

* Witness Statements
* Affidavits
* Complaints
* Deposition Transcripts
* Investigation Notes

The objective is to convert unstructured legal text into structured litigation intelligence.

---

# Step 2 – Fact Extraction

CrossEx AI identifies material facts contained within the witness narrative.

Examples include:

* Dates
* Times
* Locations
* Events
* Actions
* Observations

### Example

Input:

"I arrived at the intersection at 8 PM and observed the collision."

Extracted Facts:

* Arrival Time: 8 PM
* Location: Intersection
* Observation: Collision

These facts become the foundation for subsequent analysis.

---

# Step 3 – Timeline Analysis

The extracted facts are organized into a chronological sequence.

Timeline analysis helps lawyers understand:

* Event order
* Missing events
* Timing inconsistencies
* Narrative structure

The goal is to identify areas that require deeper questioning.

---

# Step 4 – Contradiction Detection

CrossEx AI evaluates the witness narrative for inconsistencies.

The system searches for:

* Conflicting statements
* Timeline discrepancies
* Ambiguous assertions
* Missing information
* Credibility concerns

### Example

Statement 1:

"I arrived at the location at 8 PM."

Statement 2:

"I reached the location around 7:45 PM."

Potential Issue:

Timeline inconsistency detected.

---

# Step 5 – Cross-Examination Question Generation

Once inconsistencies are identified, CrossEx AI generates targeted questions.

Question categories include:

### Fact Confirmation

Verifies factual assertions.

### Timeline Verification

Tests sequence and timing.

### Credibility Testing

Challenges conflicting statements.

### Contradiction-Focused Questions

Explores identified inconsistencies.

### Example

Generated Question:

"You previously stated that you arrived at the location at 8 PM. Why did you later state that you arrived around 7:45 PM?"

---

# Step 6 – Lawyer Review

Human review is a mandatory stage.

Lawyers may:

* Approve questions
* Edit questions
* Remove questions
* Add additional questions

CrossEx AI functions as an assistive workflow rather than a decision-making system.

All litigation decisions remain under lawyer control.

---

# Step 7 – Export

Approved question sets can be exported for trial preparation.

Export formats may include:

* Text Documents
* Trial Notes
* Question Lists
* Case Preparation Materials

The exported output becomes a ready-to-use cross-examination preparation resource.

# Design Philosophy

CrossEx AI follows five principles:

1. Focus on one workflow.
2. Keep lawyers in control.
3. Produce reviewable outputs.
4. Support explainable analysis.
5. Deliver practical litigation value.
